05/04/201744 ORDER granting 43 Letter Motion for Extension of Time. Defendants have indicated that rather than renew their motion to dismiss as against plaintiff's proposed amended complaint (or modify it as a result of the proposed amended complaint), they wish instead to oppose plaintiff's motion to amend her complaint. They do not propose any time frame by which to file their opposition. Accordingly, the Court will give the Defendants until May 12 to file any opposition to the motion. Defendants should be mindful that, in this Circuit, a pro se complaint should not be dismissed without the Court granting leave to amend at least once when a liberal reading of the complaint gives any indication that a valid claim might be stated. Dolan v. Connolly, 794 F.3d 290, 295 (2d Cir. 2015) (internal quotations and alterations omitted). Moreover, Rule 15(a) of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure mandates that leave to amend should be freely give[n]... when justice so requires. [T]his mandate is to be heeded. Foman v. Davis, 371 U.S. 178, 182 (1962). Here, this would be Plaintiff's first amendment of her pleading. Accordingly, Defendants should make clear in their opposition why they are taking the position that the motion to amend should not be granted, rather than agreeing to the filing of an amended complaint without prejudice to a renewed motion to dismiss if Defendants believe the amended complaint is deficient (or alternatively filing an answer to the amended complaint). Defendants are directed to provide a copy of this text-only order to the pro se Plaintiff forthwith. (HEREBY ORDERED by Magistrate Judge James L. Cott)(Text Only Order) Copies of Notice of Electronic Filing To Be Mailed By Clerks Office. (Cott, James) (Entered: 05/04/2017)
