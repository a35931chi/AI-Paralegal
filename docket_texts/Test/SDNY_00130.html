09/30/201460 ORDER denying 57 Letter Motion for Adjournment of Settlement Conference. The request to cancel the settlement conference scheduled for October 9 is respectfully denied. The Court believes that the parties should make their best efforts to settle this case at this juncture, before deposition discovery proceeds. The Court is mindful of plaintiff's concern that Ms. Kuczkir's participation from South Carolina may make things more difficult, and, to that end, the Court is working with her counsel to see if it is feasible for Ms. Kuczkir to participate via a video teleconference. In any event, the parties and their counsel are reminded of two important principles in anticipation of the settlement conference. First, as noted in my Standing Order for Settlement, "You never really understand a person until you consider things from his point of view - until you climb into his skin and walk around in it." (from Harper Lee's "To Kill A Mockingbird") The parties and their counsel should make all efforts to see the case through the other side's eyes as they prepare for the conference. Second, as President Lincoln once exhorted: "Discourage litigation. Persuade your neighbors to compromise whenever you can. Point out to them how the nominal winner is often a real loser - in fees, expenses, and waste of time. As a peacemaker, the lawyer has a superior opportunity of being a good man.
