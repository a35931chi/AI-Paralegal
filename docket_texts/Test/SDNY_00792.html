04/28/201528 OPINION of USCA as to 27 Corrected Notice of Appeal filed by Benihana of Tokyo, LLC USCA Case Number 14-0841. Defendantappellant Benihana of Tokyo, LLC appeals from a February 26, 2014 order of the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York (Paul A. Engelmayer, J.) granting the application of plaintiffappellee Benihana, Inc. for a preliminary injunction in aid of arbitration of a dispute arising under the parties license agreement. The district court enjoined Benihana of Tokyo from: (1) selling unauthorized food items at the restaurant it operates pursuant to the license agreement; (2) using certain trademarks in connection with the restaurant in a manner not approved by the license agreement; and (3) arguing to the arbitral panel, if it rules that Benihana of Tokyo breached the license agreement, that Benihana of Tokyo should be given additional time to cure any defaults. We conclude that the district court was within its discretion in granting the first and second components of the injunction. However, the district court erred in restricting the arguments Benihana of Tokyo may make to the arbitral panel. Catherine O'Hagan Wolfe, Clerk USCA for the Second Circuit. Certified: 04/28/2015. (nd) (Entered: 04/28/2015)
05/19/201529 MANDATE of USCA (Certified Copy) as to 27 Corrected Notice of Appeal filed by Benihana of Tokyo, LLC. USCA Case Number 14-0841. The appeal in the above captioned case from an order of the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York was argued on the district court's record and the parties' briefs. Upon consideration thereof, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED, ADJUDGED and DECREED that the order of the district court is AFFIRMED insofar as it enjoins the Appellant, pending resolution of the arbitration, from selling hamburgers or other unauthorized menu items or engaging in unapproved advertising at its Hawaii location. The order is REVERSED insofar as it enjoins the Appellant from arguing to the arbitrators for an extended cure period. Catherine O'Hagan Wolfe, Clerk USCA for the Second Circuit. 07/15/201654 ORDER denying 33 Motion for Sanctions. For that reason, BI's motion for sanctions for violations of the Preliminary Injunction is denied. Because the Court denies that motion, the Court denies BI's request for attorneys' fees incurred in litigating this motion as well. At the same time, the Court denies BOT's request for attorneys' fees in defending against BI's motion. That is because BI's bid for coercive sanctions, although resolved at a time when such sanctions were no longer viable, had a colorable, if not a compelling, basis at the time it was made. Notwithstanding its denial of BI's motion for sanctions for violations of the Preliminary Injunction as moot, the Court observes the following. In pertinent respects, the substance of the Preliminary Injunction has been recapitulated in the Permanent Injunction in 15 Civ. 7428 that took effect today when the Court adopted the arbitral panel's Award. And the parties have each recognized the Court's authority to enforce the Permanent Injunction, including by, if necessary, imposing coercive sanctions. See BI Supp. Br. 3-5; BOT Supp. Br. 2-7. The Court is hopeful that the parties will use this moment as a pivot, and attempt in good faith to repair their working relationship and amicably resolve the issues that have divided them. The disputes between BI and BOT have consumed far too much money, time, ink, and acrimony. Pending any renegotiation, however, the License Agreement governs the parties' relationship as it relates to the Benihana restaurant in Honolulu. In the event of a future motion to enforce the Court's Permanent Injunction-including based on BOT's use of advertisements, publicity, and signs that have not been approved by BI in accordance with Article 5.2 of the agreement-the Court's intention would be to resolve any such dispute with firmness and dispatch. Lest the point be unclear: The Court expects each party to punctiliously abide by the provisions of the agreement incorporated in the Permanent Injunction. The Clerk of Court is directed to close the motion pending at docket number 33 and to close this case. (As further set forth in this Order) (Signed by Judge Paul A.
