04/19/201730 ORDER: The Court is in receipt of Defense Attorney Martin Shell's request to be relieved as counsel dated April 10, 2017. (ECF No. 29.) He alleges he has been unable to communicate with his client. Corporations cannot appear pro se and must be represented by an attorney. See, e.g., Rowland v. Cal. Mens Colony, Unit II Men's Advisory Council, 506 U.S. 194, 201-02 (1993). Defendant has 60 days from the date of this Order to obtain a new attorney. Defendant is hereby put on notice that its failure to obtain new counsel will not be a defense against entry of default judgment against it. See, e.g., Premier Fla. Auto Sales & Leasing v. Mercedes-Benz of Massapequa, LLP, No. CV 10-4428 DRH WDW, 2012 WL 7149418, at *2 (E.D.N.Y. Nov. 5, 2012), report and recommendation adopted sub nom. Premier Fla. Auto Sales & Leasing, LLC v. Mercedes-Benz of Massapequa, LLC, No. 10 CV 4428 DRH WDW, 2013 WL 593506 (E.D.N.Y. Feb. 14, 2013) (entering default against an unrepresented corporate defendant and noting "[a] failure by a corporate defendant to obtain counsel constitutes a failure to defend because a corporation cannot proceed pro se."). Mr. Shell is directed to forward a copy of this Order to Defendant and to furnish the Court with all contact information for Defendant. (Signed by Judge Deborah A. Batts on 4/19/2017) (ap) (Entered: 04/19/2017)
